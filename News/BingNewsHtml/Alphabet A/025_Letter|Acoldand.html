    Letter | A cold and uncomfortable Thanksgiving dining out

    Share this:

    • Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)
    • Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)
    • Click to print (Opens in new window)
    By Letters to the Editor | Santa Cruz Sentinel
    PUBLISHED: December 2, 2024 at 2:15 PM PST

    More than a year ago I went with my family to the brunch at Chaminade for Easter. It was terrific. So when I saw an ad in the Sentinel for Thanksgiving dinner at Chaminade I signed up for six adults and one child. When we arrived at 4:15, we were shown to our table outside with a heater at one end of the table for 12. I quickly realized it would be too cold to sit outside comfortably. I was told that there were no indoor tables available. I tried to get some food but many of the stations were running out and could only come up with a small piece of turkey and some mashed potatoes. Finally I went in search for a manager. She asked my party what they wanted. My sister-in-law said another heater would help. Meanwhile the family next to us had managed to get blankets which they were wrapping around themselves. By this point we were pretty much done and ready to leave.

    We spent over $700 to shiver on our cold plastic chairs.

    — Margaret Kinstler, Soquel

Letter-writers should include their full name as well as a street address and telephone number. We don’t publish those details in the newspaper, but need the information for verification purposes. Occasionally, we reject letters simply because we’ve had so many on the same subject. Submit your letters online at www.santacruzsentinel.com/submit-letters.
