Bigfoot Beverages retracts union recognition amid ongoing 70-day strike

by KVAL

Mon, December 2nd 2024 at 2:27 PM
Updated Mon, December 2nd 2024 at 3:20 PM
(File/SBG)

    TOPICS:

    Bigfoot BeveragesUnion recognitionStrikeTeamsters Local No. 324Collective bargainingUnfair labor practiceWage increasePension funds

EUGENE, Ore. — Bigfoot Beverages said it has withdrawn its recognition of the union representing employees who have been on strike for more than 70 days.

The announcement was made via a letter dated Nov. 26 and addressed to employees in Eugene, Coos Bay, Newport, and Springfield.

A copy of the letter, which has not been independently verified by KVAL, was provided by a worker on strike.

It states the company received a petition from a majority of its employees Nov. 25 requesting to no longer be represented by Teamsters Local No. 324 and 206. In addition to not recognizing the union in contract negotiations, the company also says it has terminated its collective bargaining agreement and is halting contributions to existing pension funds.

The union called Bigfoot Beverages’ declaration an unlawful act of union busting.

“We’re waiting, you know, to hear anything from the company, and the first thing we hear is you’re decertifying the union without any recognition from the employees,” union representative Edwin Powell said. “You tell everybody that you have enough votes from the employees, but all your employees are out on the line. So, what they’re doing is – it’s a sham.”

In a press release Monday, the union echoed claims that the company’s move to no longer recognize the union was illegal.

“Bigfoot owners Eric Forrest and Andy Moore have shamelessly claimed scabs have the same status as Teamsters, who maintain their lawful work stoppage after Bigfoot forced the now months-long strike,” said Geoff Steward, Secretary-Treasurer of Local 206. “Bigfoot’s desperate attempt to use temporary replacement workers to try to decertify the union is illegal and frankly embarrassing for the company.”

The union also announced it has filed another charge of unfair labor practice with the National Labor Relations Board. The union said it has filed eight such charges since the strike began.

In the company’s letter, Bigfoot Beverages details its “Last, Best and Final Offer,” providing an average wage increase of approximately $3.14 per hour for 11 positions, with an additional $2 increase for those serving in weekend lead positions. It also provides a $7.44 increase for box truck drivers. A $2,500 bonus is listed for distribution on Dec. 6.

The company says it first made this offer Sept. 13.

Bigfoot Beverages workers have been on strike since Sept. 19. The unions cited the company’s attempt to replace employee pensions and replace it with 401k plans as the primary reason for the strike, also claiming the company had “lied to the bargaining committee during contract negotiations,” according to post on the union’s Facebook page.

The Teamsters union issued the following statement:

The company claims that it received ‘a petition from a majority of the bargaining unit.’ There are no members of Teamsters Local 206 or 324 we are aware of who signed this petition. The “facts” the employer is pointing to are insufficient to withdraw recognition. Furthermore, the Bigfoot workers have not filed a de-certification petition with the NLRB, which is what would be required if this process to move forward in the real world.
It's sad that Bigfoot is pulling fake stunts like this instead of giving the Teamsters the contract that they deserve.
