Letter: Americans will regret electing a man who attempted a coup

  • Nov 10, 2024
  • Nov 10, 2024
  • 0
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • WhatsApp
  • SMS
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

Theres a lot to process about Donald Trumps election victory last week. Economic concerns and durable dissatisfaction are complex issues poorly addressed by the Democratic Party and the Kamala Harris campaign, especially amongst voter groups they took for granted. There are equally complex critiques of the efficacy behind Trumps policy proposals.

But those critiques must come downstream of a clean and straightforward truth that paraphrases Ta Nehisi Coates assessment of Trumps first win in 2016: Certainly not every Trump voter is a racist, sexist, homophobe, transphobe, xenophobe, insurrectionist, or convicted felon. But this is not exculpatory; every single 2024 Trump voter felt it acceptable to hand the fate of the country over to one.

That assessment is not media bias or leftist alarmism. It's based on Trump's own words and actions, recorded on camera and in writing over and over and over again. He is who he says he is. Hell do what he says hell do.

People are also reading…

  • Former St. Louis Public Schools superintendent starts new career
  • Hyped-up holiday event Jingle! disappoints on opening night in OFallon, Mo.
  • CBC hockey player dies after being hit by stray bullet on I-55
  • Cardinals sign former Reds opening day shortstop to minor league deal
  • 3 merged south St. Louis Catholic parishes get a new name: St. Lucy
  • While rivals shop, Cardinals trade past expectations for future considerations. But why now?
  • St. Louis school districts lose nearly 11,000 students over 5 years
  • Family blames fiery I-55 deaths of mom, daughters on lumber truck in St. Louis County suit
  • Missouri releases latest school scores. Underperforming districts get reprieve.
  • Kansas City Chiefs to leave conventional TV, Missouri basketball team finally to join it
  • Several inches of snow forecast Saturday in St. Louis. Travel may be hazardous.
  • Missouri judge dumps Trump-inspired voting regulations
  • Hochman: Mizzou fans say goodbye to Luther Burden III. Could he be highest-drafted NFL Tiger?
  • Man charged with killing Clayton woman dated her sister, wanted her money, feds say
  • Biden pardons his son Hunter despite previous pledges not to

The time will soon come when Americans will need to answer for why they elected a man who tried to overthrow the government on camera, in front of the world in order to lead it.

Ryan Bueckendorf

Clifton Heights
