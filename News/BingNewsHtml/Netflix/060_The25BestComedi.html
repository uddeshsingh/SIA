The 25 Best Comedies on Netflix to Watch Now

Have a laugh—you’ve earned it.

By Emma Carey, Josh Rosenberg and Bria McNealPublished: Nov 4, 2024

It’s finally cold enough to justify a night in, so why not have a movie night? You don’t have to brave the outdoors to have fun. Grab a snack and a drink, then pop a squat on the couch. While you’re at it, head over to Netflix. We’re embarking on, you know, a pretty stressful week—so now is a good time to remind you that the streamer has plenty of funny movies to choose from.

Below I’ve rounded up 25 options to get you through it all. There are classic buddy comedies like Superbad and The Other Guys, starring your favorite comedians, Jonah Hill, Michael Cera, Will Ferrell, and Mark Wahlberg. And if you’re in the mood for a rom-com, look no further than Anyone but You or The Lovebirds—both flicks combine a will-they-won’t-they romance with a load of hilarious hijinks. If you’d rather avoid the mush, you can watch Adam Sandler play a basketball scout in Hustle or a doting father in You Are So Not Invited to My Bat Mitzvah. Or you could skip both and meet up with Benoit Blanc instead in Glass Onion: A Knives Out Mystery.

No matter what you choose, I can guarantee you’ll have fun. I won’t hold you up any longer. Here are the best comedies on Netflix.

Glass Onion: A Knives Out Mystery

View full post on Youtube

In this hilarious sequel to Rian Johnson’s 2019 whodunit hit, Knives Out, tech billionaire Miles Bron invites his friends to his private island. The vacation kicks off well, but when one guest winds up dead, Detective Benoit Blanc (Daniel Craig) rushes to the scene. Foul play is certainly afoot, but who’s to blame?

Watch Now

Superbad

View full post on Youtube

Jonah Hill and Michael Cera star as Seth and Evan, high school buddies who are on a mission. Before graduating, they want to lose their virginities. Naturally, that proves to be more difficult than anticipated—but it makes for one hell of a journey.

Watch Now

Anchorman: The Legend of Ron Burgundy

View full post on Youtube

Does Will & Harper have you craving a classic Will Ferrell comedy? Well, it’s time for an Anchorman rewatch. The comedy great stars as Ron Burgundy, a TV anchorman who offers a job to rising reporter Veronica Corningstone (Christina Applegate). For a while, Ron feels great about welcoming a woman into the newsroom, but when Veronica outperforms him, Ron’s jealousy threatens to ruin his career.

Watch Now

The Other Guys

View full post on Youtube

Even more Ferrell! In The Other Guys, NYPD detectives Gamble (Ferrell) and Hoitz (Mark Wahlberg) jump at the opportunity to solve a high-profile crime. Here’s the catch: They’re not as coordinated, talented, or prepared as their coworkers. What could go wrong?

Watch Now

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

No Hard Feelings

View full post on Youtube

Maddie’s going to lose her house if she doesn’t do something—anything, really—to protect it. In a fit of desperation, she responds to a local ad from a couple who wants to set their 19-year-old son, Percy, up on a date. Maddie has to break the kid out of his shell to get paid, but as she and Percy get acquainted, the mission becomes hilariously complicated.

Watch Now

Anyone but You

View full post on Youtube

Anyone but You stars Glen Powell and Sydney Sweeney as Ben and Beatrice—two people whose first date didn’t work out. In a twist of fate, they wind up at the same wedding in Australia several months later. When both of their exes show up, Ben and Beatrice agree to be a fake couple. That always works out, right?

Watch Now

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

You Are So Not Invited to My Bat Mitzvah

View full post on Youtube

This heartwarming comedy comes straight from the Sandman. Adam Sandler plays Danny Friedman. Danny’s daughter Stacy (Sunny Sandler) is preparing for her bat mitzvah, but instead of taking the event seriously, she’s focused on the boy she has a crush on and other middle school drama. As her big day approaches, Stacy’s world unravels, which forces her to question what being an adult really means.

Watch Now

Hustle

View full post on Youtube

Adam Sandler teams up with real-life pro basketball player Juancho Hernangómez for this comedy/drama about a talent scout who works to break an international player out of obscurity and turn him into a star.

Watch Now

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Pee-wee’s Big Holiday

View full post on Youtube

Pee-wee Herman, a quirky and eccentric vaudeville-type character created by the late comedian Paul Reubens, goes on vacation in Pee-wee’s Big Holiday. Released on Netflix in 2016, it was the zany character’s second sequel to Tim Burton’s excellent Pee-wee’s Big Adventure in 1985.

Watch Now

We Have a Ghost

View full post on Youtube

A suburban family becomes an overnight sensation after capturing footage of a ghost in their home. But shit hits the fan when they try to uncover how the spirit died, making them the newest target of the CIA.

Watch Now

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Bo Burnham: Inside

View full post on Youtube

Technically, Burnham’s pandemic creation—his personal King Lear—is a stand-up special. But as any fan of the comedian will tell you (and probably already has told you many times), there’s a lot more to it than that. In many ways, the musical-comedy special feels like a feature film, as Burnham muses about our world from the inside out.

Watch Now

I Care a Lot

View full post on Youtube

Rosamund Pike stars in this dark comedy as a professional legal guardian who, unbeknownst to her clients, is legally siphoning off their money for her own use. Her master scheme seems to be going without a hitch—that is until she learns that her newest client is associated with a gangster (played by Peter Dinklage).

Watch Now

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Bad Trip

View full post on Youtube

It’s Eric André doing what Eric André does best: making unassuming onlookers the audience for his over-the-top antics. Except this time around he’s trading in his signature public-access television studio for the real world. In this hidden-camera comedy, Chris and Bud (André and actor Lil Rel Howery) are best friends who take a a cross-country trip to help Chris confess his feelings for his high school crush. However, hot on their trail is Chris’s sister (played by Tiffany Haddish), from whom he has “borrowed” the car for their trip.

Watch Now

Always Be My Maybe

View full post on Youtube

Ali Wong and Randall Park are a perfect duo in this heartwarming rom-com about two childhood friends whose awkward fling is reignited by a later-in-life reunion. Back in their hometown digs of San Francisco, the two must face whether their separate worlds can find a way to intersect the way they used to.

Watch Now

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Hasan Minhaj: Homecoming King

View full post on Youtube

Before the world knew him from Patriot Act, many knew Hasan Minhaj from his work on The Daily Show and, of course, his debut stand-up special, Homecoming King. The latter is a quintessential preview of Minhaj’s tactful skills as a comedian, as he covers racism, immigration, and his experience as an Indian American in a way that is personal, impactful, and entertaining all at once.

Watch Now

Hunt for the Wilderpeople

View full post on Youtube

Taika Waititi is undoubtedly an “it” director right now, with films like the Oscar-winning Jojo Rabbit, Thor: Love and Thunder, and What We Do in the Shadows under his belt. If you’re a fan of the Kiwi auteur’s work, you should definitely check out 2016’s Hunt for the Wilderpeople. The charming and eccentric adventure comedy, set in the New Zealand wilderness, follows 13-year-old Ricky Baker as he flees from the law with his foster father.

Watch Now

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Eurovision Song Contest: The Story of Fire Saga

View full post on Youtube

Will Ferrell and Rachel McAdams star in this daffy musical comedy about two small-town Icelandic musicians who travel across the world to chase their dreams of pop stardom at the global phenomenon known as Eurovision. If you’re a Eurovision newbie, the toe-tapping soundtrack and onstage mishaps will enthrall you, while established fans will be delighted by Easter eggs like cameos from favorite contestants.

Watch Now

Between Two Ferns: The Movie

View full post on Youtube

Zach Galifianakis’s offbeat comedy series gets the feature-length treatment here: Between Two Ferns goes on a nationwide redemption tour in the wake of a disastrous near-death incident involving interviewee Matthew McConaughey. Galifianakis’s deadpan, painfully awkward shtick is as appealing as ever in the gut-busting road-trip comedy.

Watch Now

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

Set It Up

View full post on Youtube

In a twist on the whole setup narrative, two overworked N.Y.C. assistants try to set up their successful yet overbearing bosses—who are played by Lucy Liu and Taye Diggs.

Watch Now

Dolemite Is My Name

View full post on Youtube

This biographical film centers on struggling comedian Rudy Ray Moore in the 1970s as he creates his raunchy alter ego, Dolemite, and risks it all to take his act to the big screen with an anti-establishment kung fu film.

Watch Now
